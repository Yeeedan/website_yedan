Chinese Typeface
</br>Intership in 3type
</br>Summer 2025


The history of typography is inextricably linked to writing tools — whether it's the brush, the chisel, or the pen, each leaves its stylistic mark on the glyph. For me, vector graphics are a primary medium of creation. I love the beauty in the digital process: clicking two points to create a line, and adjusting curves with the movement of a cursor.

In this project, I extracted the skeletal structures from ancient Chinese woodblock prints, stripping away their traditional stroke characteristics to create a minimalist, monolinear typeface. During my internship at 3type, a studio deeply involved in the localization of type design software, I learned their systematic methodology for managing vast character sets (CJK). By applying parametric principles to categorize stroke density and spacing (horizontal vs. vertical, convergence vs. dispersion), I focused on controlling the typographic grayness for optimal legibility. This experience has profoundly expanded my understanding of multiscript type design.
