Magazines, editorial materials, cardboard display stands
</br>Graduation project, Guided by ZHENG Chao, Work with Li Xinyi, Xia Xinyu, Ye Yao
</br>CAA, Hangzhou, 2024

<i>KAKA</i> is a year-long social observation and creative publishing experiment using cake as a medium. Through seven issues, we transformed cake, an everyday food, into a vehicle for exploring cultural memory, emotional connections, and social issues. Each issue approaches the topic from a different perspective—whether it's the symbolism of cake in festive rituals, fragments of personal life stories, or observations of cake shops as a third space in the city—using the approachability of food to soften the seriousness of social observation and inviting readers to savor the flavors of life.

The exhibition is a crucial part of this project. Inspired by numerous book fairs, we collaborated with a factory to design unique, environmentally friendly cardboard exhibition stands. While flipping through the magazines, visitors also left their own cake stories and associations. These unexpected interactions echoed the project's original intention: in an age of information overload, moments as real and vivid as sharing a cake may be a gentle form of resistance to maintain our senses.
